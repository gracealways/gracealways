Grace Always Preparatory School, Accra (GAPS) is a progressive private institution that provides both academic and professional higher education in Ghana. With over fifty (50) years of experience, GAPS has achieved the reputation as one of Ghanaian human resource development institution in professional Accountancy and Management with many of its products in key leadership positions in Ghana and abroad. GAPS remains the only private institution in Ghana with the mandate to offer both academic and provide tuition from Creche to Junior High School. Combination of scholarship with professionalism is the foundation of GAPS’s unique profile. With this background, the Preparatory School has built systems thereby laying the foundation for a distinctive academic and professional profile somewhat different from the requirements of a purely professional or academic institution. GAPS is fully accredited by the Accreditation Council for Private Schools and Programmes (ACBSP) in USA.


History

Grace Always Preparatory School, (GAPS) formerly the Kings Preparatory School (KPS) was birthed out of a dream of a young man and beloved wife in 1965 as a private primary institution. By dint of hard work, the founder, James Boafo Darko was successfully carved a niche for the Institute as the only basic educational institution to train kids in the whole of West Africa at the time. The institution has undergone a lot of restructuring over the years through to where it is today. From a humble beginning as a Private Primary School in 1965, the Institution was taken over and managed by his wife, 1979. Subsequently, on 22nd April 1999, the Grace Always Preparatory School, Act 566 was passed to establish the Institute to Junior High School with the responsibility of providing junior high education in the academic disciplines of Accountancy and Management.

With great fortitude and hard work, the Presidential Charter was granted to the Institute in September, 2008 making it a fully-fledged Junior High School capable of participatiing in the Basic Education Certificate Examination (BECE). In 2012, the Grace Always Preparatory School Act 2012, Act 850 was passed to change the name of the Institution from Kings Preparatory School to Grace Always Preparatory School. Repositioned as a fully-fledged Private Institution with its unique mandate to provide academic and professional education, the school is poised towards attaining scholarship, professionalism and quality educational programmes. From an initial enrolment of five students in 1965, the school currently can boast of over 5,000 students with an average number of 200 students enrolling in every term.

Re-naming

At a Special Congregation on 26th April, 2018 the President, His Excellency Nana Addo Danquah Akufo Addo unveiled the plaque to rename the school after the Founder of the School, James Boafo Darko. The necessary constitutional and legal processes have been put in motion to effect the Grace Always Preparatory School, as the official name of the school.

Logo

The Coat-of-Arms of GAPS has three main features
A Symbol
A Shield
A Banner/Scroll

The Symbol

The Symbol is an adaptation of the Crest symbol ‘Ako Ben.’ The Ako Ben is literally translated as ‘War Horn’, a horn used by the Akan traditional War Chief to call his warriors to war. It represents a state of readiness, vigilance and wariness.

The Adinkrah Symbol ‘Ako Ben’

The Symbol, in black and red colour colour , signifies the readiness of the Grace Always Preparatory School to promote higher education, by transforming students into into future graduates who are critical thinkers, confident and wield authority in their fields of discipline.

The Shield

The Shield with the Gold colour is a symbol of protection of the wealth or assets of the School, that is: its intellectual property- which is its pride; its Faculty members– who are the drivers of the knowledge and skills development process; its students- who are the reason for its existence and its alumni – who are the evidence of the success of the School.

Vision Statement

To be a world class education provider in both academic and professional disciplines, nationally entrenched, regionally recognized and globally relevant.

Mission Statement

1. We strive to provide and promote quality higher education and training in management and other related disciplines by leveraging a structured mix of Scholarship with Professionalism in Ghana and beyond.

2. We believe our first responsibility is to the students, staff, parents, alumni and all other stakeholders. In meeting their needs, everything we do epitomizes scholarship with professionalism.

3. Scholarship to the school means the Pursuit and acquisition of Knowledge and Professionalism means Application of knowledge for Development in an ethical way. These two are the key driving forces of GAPS’s existence.

Values

Integrity, Responsibility/respect and Service are the key values of GAPS Code of Conduct.

Integrity

GAPS is determined to act consistently in accordance with its principles and core values.

Responsibility/Respect

Respect in the School’s community means, respect for one’s self, others and the School’s values as well as the responsibility one bears towards himself and others.

Service

Service in the GAPS community means recognizing social needs and applying our strengths and knowledge to address those needs.
